Urgent Civic Appeal: Strategic Investment in Cape Town’s Unseen Heroes

To the Leadership of the Democratic Alliance and the Western Cape Government,

I write to you today not as a politician or an academic, but as a citizen who has lived across the full spectrum of Cape Town’s realities. I have experienced the despair of homelessness and drug addiction, and I have spent time in holding cells. I have also lived the quiet life of the suburbs and held corporate roles at some of South Africa’s largest companies.

This journey has given me a unique vantage point. From the boardroom to the streets, I have witnessed firsthand the true engine that keeps our city running.

During my darkest days, it was not formal institutions that saved my life. It was the "garbage entrepreneurs" who showed me the value of honest work. It was the spiritual leaders and mentors—operating in the shadows of prisons and on the hardest streets—who offered guidance when I had none. It was the grassroots community groups who stepped in where the system fell short.

Every single day, these unsung community members are actively doing the heavy lifting of civic duty. They are:

  • Turning our prisons into genuine spaces of rehabilitation.
  • Cleaning our streets and maintaining our public spaces.
  • Managing recycling and waste, driving our informal green economy.
  • Protecting our children in vulnerable neighborhoods.
  • Preventing crime and weaving the social fabric that strengthens our communities.

Yet, the vast majority of these individuals perform this grueling, essential work without compensation, recognition, or support. This must change, and it must change urgently.

Providing financial support and structural backing to these grassroots heroes is not an act of charity. It is a critical, strategic investment in the stability, safety, and future prosperity of Cape Town. When we fund the people who are already doing the work, we amplify their impact exponentially. We formalize a shadow economy of care and protection that our city desperately relies upon.

This matter requires the highest possible level of urgency from your leadership. These individuals and community groups are not just well-meaning volunteers; they are Cape Town’s last line of defense against social collapse.

I urge you to take immediate, decisive action to formalize, fund, and support these grassroots initiatives. Let us invest in the people who have already invested their lives in saving our city.
